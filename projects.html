Honors Thesis — Multi-Agent Control via Graph Neural Networks

Nonlinear Controls and Robotics (NCR) Laboratory · Ongoing Honors Thesis

I am currently conducting my Honors Thesis research in the Nonlinear Controls and Robotics (NCR) Laboratory, focusing on the development of a multi-agent control framework based on Graph Neural Networks (GNNs). The project investigates how graph-based learning architectures can be integrated with control theory to enable scalable, decentralized coordination among multiple autonomous agents.

My work involves modeling agent interactions using graph representations, designing learning-based controllers that respect system dynamics, and evaluating performance in simulation environments relevant to robotics and autonomous systems. This research aims to improve robustness and scalability in multi-agent coordination tasks such as formation control and cooperative navigation.
